4) Water Consumption Anomaly Detector
Problem
  • Before quarterly bills, analysts manually scan usage to catch leaks (spikes) or dead meters/batteries (drops).
  • Reviewing thousands of accounts to find a few true issues is inefficient.
Technical Solution
  • Historical pattern analysis: Analyzes account-level consumption history to identify meters with previous leaks or dead batteries, examining cases where consumption values were significantly higher or lower than normal patterns.
  • Supervised learning implementation: Trains scikit-learn models using historical confirmed leaks and meter failures, incorporating consumption patterns, meter age, property characteristics, and seasonal variations to predict potential issues.
  • Unsupervised anomaly detection: Employs Isolation Forest algorithms to perform statistical anomaly detection, identifying unusual consumption patterns without requiring labeled training data by isolating data points that differ significantly from the majority.
  • Multi-algorithm ensemble: Combines results from both supervised and unsupervised learning approaches, applying additional filtering based on account details (meter age, ownership changes, seasonal patterns, neighborhood averages) to reduce false positives and improve detection accuracy.
  • Intelligent reporting: Generates comprehensive Excel outputs with flagged accounts, confidence scores, consumption patterns, and recommended actions, enabling city staff to efficiently identify and contact affected property owners.
